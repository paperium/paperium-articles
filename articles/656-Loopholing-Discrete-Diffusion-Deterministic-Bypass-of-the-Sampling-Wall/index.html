Short Review

Overview

The article presents a novel approach to discrete diffusion models through the introduction of Loopholing Discrete Diffusion Models (LDDMs). The primary goal is to address the inherent "sampling wall" problem, which leads to the collapse of rich distributional information into one-hot vectors during categorical sampling. By employing a deterministic latent pathway and a self-conditioning training strategy, LDDMs significantly enhance generative performance, achieving a reduction in generative perplexity by up to 61% compared to previous models. Additionally, LDDMs demonstrate improved performance in reasoning tasks, such as arithmetic benchmarks, indicating their potential for high-quality non-autoregressive text generation.
