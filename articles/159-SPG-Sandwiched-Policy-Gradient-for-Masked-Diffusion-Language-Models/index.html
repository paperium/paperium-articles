Overview

The article presents a novel approach to enhancing diffusion large language models (dLLMs) through a new reinforcement learning (RL) algorithm known as the Sandwiched Policy Gradient (SPG). This method addresses the challenges of aligning dLLMs with human preferences by optimizing both upper and lower bounds of log-likelihood, which is crucial for effective model training. The authors demonstrate that SPG significantly outperforms existing RL methods, achieving notable accuracy improvements across various benchmarks, including GSM8K, MATH500, Countdown, and Sudoku. The study emphasizes the importance of a block-wise masking strategy and a mixture of evidence upper and lower bounds for enhancing model performance.
