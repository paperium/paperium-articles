Short Review

Advancing Multilingual LLM Watermarking Robustness with STEAM

This insightful article addresses a critical challenge in the field of Large Language Model (LLM) watermarking: the significant failure of existing multilingual methods, particularly in low-resource languages. The authors meticulously demonstrate that current approaches, despite claims of cross-lingual robustness, falter under translation attacks due to inherent limitations in semantic clustering. This vulnerability stems from insufficient full-word token coverage within tokenizers for underrepresented languages. To overcome this, the paper introduces STEAM (Simple Translation-Enhanced Approach for Multilingual watermarking), a novel back-translation-based detection method. Through rigorous evaluation across 17 diverse languages, STEAM showcases remarkable improvements in watermark strength, achieving average gains of +0.19 AUC and +40%p TPR@1%, thereby offering a robust and fairer path toward equitable LLM traceability.

Its demonstrated robustness across 17 languages, including unsupported ones and under various translation attacks, provides compelling evidence of its efficacy. Furthermore, the method's non-invasive nature and extendability to new languages underscore its versatility and potential for broad adoption, promoting greater fairness in LLM output traceability.
